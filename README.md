# SQL Business Case Studies

A collection of SQL-based business case studies designed to solve real-world analytics problems using structured query techniques and business-oriented thinking.

---

- SQL Query Writing
- Aggregations
- Joins
- Subqueries
- Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
- Window Functions

- Business Analysis
- Revenue Analysis
- Customer Analytics
- Product Analytics
- Trend Analysis

---

## Case Studies

### 01. Customer Revenue Analysis
Business Questions:
- Who are the highest-value customers?
- How much revenue comes from the top 10%, 20%, and 50% of customers?
- Is the business exposed to customer concentration risk?

Key Insight:
- Top 20% customers generate nearly 48% of total revenue.

---

### 02. Regional Sales Analysis
Business Questions:
- Which regions generate the highest revenue?
- Which region has the highest average order value?
- How does customer spending differ across regions?

Key Insight:
- East region has the highest average revenue per order despite lower total revenue than West.

---

### 03. Product Mix Analysis
Business Questions:
- Which product categories drive revenue?
- Why do some regions generate higher order values?
- How does product mix impact performance?

Key Insight:
- Technology products are the primary driver of high-value orders.

---

### 04. Seasonal Sales Analysis
Business Questions:
- How has revenue changed over time?
- Are there recurring seasonal patterns?
- Which months perform best and worst?

Key Insight:
- Q4 consistently generates the strongest sales performance.

---

### 05. Geographic Revenue Analysis
Business Questions:
- Which states generate the highest revenue?
- Is revenue concentrated in a small number of markets?
- What geographic risks exist?

Key Insight:
- Top 5 states contribute more than 50% of total revenue.
